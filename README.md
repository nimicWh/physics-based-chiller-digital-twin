## Overview

The **Physics-Based Chiller Digital Twin** is a proof-of-concept engineering project developed to simulate the operation and performance of industrial chiller systems.

Unlike simplified calculators or purely data-driven models, this project focuses on **physics-based equipment modelling**, enabling realistic simulation of system behaviour, operating conditions, equipment performance, and engineering KPIs.

The project demonstrates the application of software engineering principles to industrial automation and HVAC systems, serving as a project in **Digital Twin**, **Industrial Digitalisation (Industry 4.0)**.

---

# Features

## Physics-Based Simulation

* refrigeration cycle simulation
* Refrigerant calculations
* Compressor modelling
* Evaporator simulation
* Condenser simulation
* Expansion valve simulation
* Dynamic operating condition updates

---

## System Performance Analytics

Real-time calculation of engineering performance indicators including:

* Cooling Capacity
* Compressor Power
* Plant Power Consumption
* Equipment Efficiency
* System Operating Status

---

## Equipment Monitoring

Live monitoring of major chiller components:

* Compressor
* Evaporator
* Condenser
* Expansion Valve
* Pumps
* Chilled Water Circuit
* Condenser Water Circuit

Typical operating parameters include:

* Temperature
* Pressure
* Refrigerant Flow
* Water Flow
* Electrical Power
* Compressor Speed
* Equipment Load
* Operating Efficiency

---

## Equipment Health Monitoring

Condition monitoring capabilities include:

* Equipment Health Score
* Performance Degradation Tracking
* Remaining Useful Life (RUL) Estimation (not done yet)
* Equipment Status Assessment
* Maintenance Indicators

---

## Alarm & Fault Simulation

Simulation of common operating abnormalities including:

* High Pressure
* Low Pressure
* High Temperature
* Compressor Overload
* Refrigerant Fault Conditions
* Equipment Warning States

---

## Interactive Dashboard

The application provides an industrial-style monitoring interface featuring:

* Analog engineering gauges
* Live operating values
* Equipment status indicators
* KPI cards
* Historical trend charts
* Alarm notifications
* Real-time simulation updates
